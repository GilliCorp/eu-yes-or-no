## BOTTOM LINE

EU membership would **not** automatically remove, ban, or make verðtrygging obsolete — not in the
short or medium term. EU/EEA consumer-credit law *regulates* indexation (transparency/fairness) but
does **not** prohibit it, and that law **already applies in Iceland via the EEA**, so membership
changes little legally. The real lever is the **euro**, which is a multi-year path (ERM II + Maastricht
criteria) whose preconditions (durable low inflation, converged rates) are the very things that erode
verðtrygging's rationale. Meanwhile, **the bigger live pressure on verðtrygging right now is domestic**
— 2025 Supreme Court rulings, banks pausing indexed loans, and a government plan to shrink their weight
from 2027 — all independent of the EU. And it's all newly topical because **Iceland has a referendum on
29 Aug 2026 on resuming EU accession talks.**

## PART A — The EU/legal question (Pass 1, fully verified)

### Today (already true under the EEA)
- ✅ EU/EEA law does **not ban** indexation; it regulates it via transparency. Unfair Terms Directive
  **93/13/EEC** is expressly *"without hindrance to price-indexation clauses, where lawful, provided
  that the method by which prices vary is explicitly described."* Mortgage Credit Directive
  **2014/17/EU** Art. 24 permits index-linked credit if the index is "clear, accessible, objective and
  verifiable."
- ✅ **All this law already binds Iceland through the EEA** (93/13/EEC; Consumer Credit Dir 2008/48/EC,
  implemented by Act No 33/2013; Unfair Commercial Practices 2005/29/EC; MCD 2014/17/EU "Text with EEA
  relevance"). So EU membership introduces **no new legal regime** for verðtrygging.
- ✅ Litigated and **not** struck down: EFTA Court **E-25/13** (Engilbertsson v Íslandsbanki) and
  **E-27/13** (Gunnarsson v Landsbankinn), both 2014 — Court referred the fairness assessment back to
  Icelandic courts rather than ruling indexation unlawful.
- ✅ **EFTA Surveillance Authority** (25 Feb 2013): Consumer Credit Directive doesn't even apply to
  mortgages; 93/13 doesn't prohibit indexation. **Central Bank of Iceland**: it is *"far-fetched to
  expect indexation to be deemed illegal."*
- ✅ Legal today, conditioned on **Act No 38/2001** (Interest & Price Indexation) + EEA disclosure
  rules. The only adverse EEA finding was about **disclosure methodology** (computing APR on a 0%-inflation
  assumption), not a ban.

### The euro path (why "join EU ≠ kill verðtrygging")
- ✅ **EU membership ≠ instant euro.** New members join with a derogation, must sit in **ERM II ≥ 2 years**
  without devaluing their central rate, then meet **Maastricht** criteria. The 2-yr clock only starts
  *after* accession (Bulgaria, Croatia took years).
- ✅ Maastricht **presupposes** durable low inflation (12-mo HICP within 1.5pp of best performers) and
  converged long-term rates — i.e. rate convergence is a **precondition** for the euro, not a gift from
  it (ECB; IoES/Univ. of Iceland).
- ✅ Inflation doesn't vanish on accession — high-inflation members "continued to have [it] for some
  time after accession" (Icelandic govt summary).
- ✅ Euro-driven low rates "evaporate" without domestic fiscal discipline; Iceland's PM said the govt
  would target the Maastricht criteria "whether or not Iceland joins the euro" (IoES; EP briefing).

### Economic rationale & the 2008 trade-off
- ✅ Verðtrygging exists because the króna chronically weakened + inflation ran high + (historically)
  negative real rates; indexation (Act 13/1979, Ólafslög) made long-term mortgages viable. After 2008
  the ISK fell >50% vs EUR (~85 → ~180), and ~80% of household debt was indexed, so inflation hit
  borrowers directly.
- ✅ Strongest **anti-euro** point: an independent, devaluable króna acted as a shock absorber in the
  2008 recovery (EP briefing, IMF/CEPR) — the trade-off being that the same devaluation worsened indexed
  debt (the strongest **pro-euro** point).

### Accession-talks record
- ✅ Verðtrygging was **not** a topic in the 2010–2013 accession negotiations. 27/33 chapters opened,
  11 provisionally closed; unfinished = fisheries, agriculture, free movement of capital,
  establishment/services. No "verðtrygging chapter."

- ❌ "The euro is not necessary to lower interest rates." (0-3)

## PART B — Current 2024–2026 status (Pass 2)

### ✅ Verified (survived fact-check before the limit hit)
- **EFTA Court E-4/23**, *Neytendastofa v Íslandsbanki*, judgment **23 May 2024** (ref. from Landsréttur):
  about **disclosure** of variable rates / how CPI-indexation feeds the APRC under Consumer Credit Dir
  2008/48/EC — **not** a ruling on whether verðtrygging itself is unfair/invalid. Held: disclosure fails
  if conditions for changing the rate are only a vague/"etc." reference to unforeseen cost increases.
  (Source: eftacourt.int E-4/23 press + judgment PDF)
- **EFTA Court E-25/25** — despite the name people cite, it is **NOT about verðtrygging**. It's about
  free movement of persons / recognition of a parent-child relationship. Pending: referred by Reykjavík
  District Court 4 Nov 2025; hearing 25 Mar 2026; no judgment yet. (eftacourt.int/cases/e-25-25)
- **Government's first housing package (29 Oct 2025)**: includes a measure to **reduce the weight of
  verðtryggð residential mortgages via minimum-repayment-rate rules, taking effect 2027.** (stjórnarráðið
  — verified 2-0) *Quote:* "Með reglum um lágmarkshraða afborgana til að minnka vægi verðtryggðra
  íbúðalána sem taki gildi á árinu 2027."

### ✅ Verified in re-verification pass (all confirmed, high confidence; nuances folded in)
**The domestic "vaxtamálið" (interest-rate case) saga — bigger live pressure on verðtrygging than the EU:**
- **14 Oct 2025 — Hæstiréttur** partially invalidated ("ógiltir að hluta") the variable-rate terms of an
  **unindexed (óverðtryggt)** Íslandsbanki loan — a test case by **Neytendasamtökin** for ~3,000
  borrowers. Banks may **not** change rates on **subjective ("huglæg") criteria**; must use objective
  benchmarks tied to the **Central Bank's reference rates.** *Nuance:* the borrower won the principle but
  the **monetary claim was rejected** (CB rate had risen more than the loan rate), so the bank paid
  nothing. (RÚV, ns.is, Heimildin, mbl)
- **After the ruling (20–21 Oct 2025) all three big banks PAUSED issuing indexed (verðtryggð) mortgages**
  citing legal uncertainty (Landsbankinn 20 Oct; Íslandsbanki + Arion 21 Oct). *Nuance:* targeted
  subtypes (esp. variable-rate indexed), not a blanket halt; banks resumed with **revised** indexed terms
  within weeks (e.g. Íslandsbanki 4.75% fixed, floor 3.5%, ceiling 5.25%). (vb.is, mbl, Heimildin, Vísir)
- **22 Dec 2025 — Hæstiréttur** ruled in the Landsbankinn cases (no. **32/2025 & 35/2025**) + Íslandsbanki:
  rate-adjustment terms **invalid except where they reference Central Bank rates**, but **banks acquitted**
  of borrowers' repayment claims. *Nuance:* the related **Arion** ruling came earlier, **10 Dec 2025**
  (claim had grouped it with the 22 Dec batch). Anchored to **§34 of the 2016 Consumer Mortgage Act**:
  rate-change criteria must be "clear, accessible, objective and verifiable." (ns.is, mbl, RÚV)
- **Govt fix:** in the **29 Oct 2025 housing package**, the govt will have the **Central Bank publish an
  interest-rate benchmark (vaxtaviðmið)** "as soon as possible" to base indexed loans on, to remove the
  ruling's market uncertainty. (stjórnarráðið, Alþingi)

**The political moment:**
- ✅ **Referendum on resuming EU accession talks: Saturday 29 Aug 2026.** Question: *"Á Ísland að hefja á
  ný aðildarviðræður við Evrópusambandið?"* (Yes/No), per **Parliamentary Resolution No. 14/157, adopted
  28 May 2026** (34 for, 8 against, 14 abstain). *Key:* it is **ADVISORY, not binding** — and a *separate*
  referendum would be needed to approve any actual accession agreement. A "No" would end the process.
  (stjórnarráðið, Wikipedia, govt.is, Brussels Signal, Arctic Portal)

**Market & macro (Seðlabanki / Hagstofa):**
- ✅ By **2022 non-indexed (óverðtryggð) loans ≈ 60%** of the household mortgage stock (record high). From
  **2023** households **shifted back to indexed** as rates rose (indexed payment burden rose less). In
  **autumn 2024** the big-3 tightened indexed terms: typical max term cut to **~25 yrs (from 40)**;
  **equal-payment (jafngreiðslu) indexed loans limited to first-time buyers.** *Nuance:* "~25 yr" is the
  sector typical (Arion 25; Landsbankinn kept 30 for general indexed loans). (Seðlabanki, Landsbankinn, Arion)
- ✅ **Inflation: 12-mo CPI 5.1% as of May 2026**; CPI index **684.3** (base May 1988=100). That May 2026
  value (684.3) is the reference applied to indexed loans for **July 2026.** (Hagstofa)
