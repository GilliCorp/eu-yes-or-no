## BOTTOM LINE

For **defence**, EU membership would add a *second* binding mutual-defence promise (Art. 42(7) TEU) on
top of Iceland's NATO Article 5 guarantee — but the EU treaties themselves call **NATO "the foundation
of collective defence,"** so for a non-military NATO founding member the addition is **modest and
largely non-duplicative, not transformative.** Iceland's security would still rest on its two pillars
(**NATO + the 1951 US defence pact**), and membership would **not** require it to build an army.

For **energy**, the famous *Orkupakki 3* fear is **legally unfounded under today's setup**: Iceland's
grid is isolated, so the EU's cross-border/ACER rules have "no tangible impact," and any interconnector
decision "lies entirely" with Iceland. **The catch:** that protection comes from the EEA's two-pillar
buffer (oversight via **ESA, not ACER**) — and **full EU membership would remove that buffer**, giving
ACER direct competence *if* an interconnector were ever built (none is planned).

The biggest real shift membership brings is **a formal seat and vote** in EU foreign/security policy,
replacing today's voluntary alignment.

## TODAY (current EEA/NATO status)
- ✅ **No standing army.** Security rests on **NATO (founding member, 1949)** + the **1951 bilateral US
  defence agreement.** Iceland keeps a Coast Guard, police, the Iceland Air Defence System, and a
  voluntary Crisis Response Unit ("no army" ≠ "no security capacity"). (Govt of Iceland; EPRS 2026; SIPRI)
- ✅ Under the EEA, Iceland has only a **"dialogue" relationship with EU foreign policy (CFSP)** and is
  **not obliged** to take part in EU defence (CSDP) — but it **voluntarily aligns** with most EU foreign-
  policy declarations and **fully applies EU sanctions on Russia.** (SIPRI; EPRS 2026; EFTA)
- ✅ **Energy: the Third Energy Package already binds Iceland via the EEA** (in force 3 Oct 2019) — not
  via membership. Because the grid is isolated: the **ACER / cross-border provisions have "no tangible
  impact"** on Icelandic sovereignty, **full control over energy resources is unaffected**, and
  **interconnector decisions "lie entirely" with Icelandic authorities.** Oversight runs through **ESA,
  not ACER** (the EEA two-pillar system). Alþingi passed Orkupakki 3 in 2019 (46–13); the opposition was
  political/precautionary, not a legal finding. (EU Commission Joint Understanding 2019; ACER; ESA)
- ✅ **Already deepening short of membership:** the **EU–Iceland Security & Defence Partnership signed
  18 March 2026** (Arctic, maritime security, Ukraine, cyber, economic security) — **non-binding**, and
  explicitly **complements rather than replaces** NATO and the US pact. (EEAS; EPRS 2026)

## AS A MEMBER (what changes)
- ✅ **Adds Art. 42(7) TEU mutual-assistance** — binding, but "consistent with commitments under NATO,
  which … will remain the foundation of collective defence," and softer in practice ("by all the means
  in their power"). A parallel guarantee, not a replacement for NATO Art. 5. (EEAS)
- ✅ **Binding CFSP/CSDP participation + a formal vote/seat** in EU foreign & security decisions,
  replacing today's voluntary alignment. The CFSP acquis = political dialogue, aligning with EU
  declarations, joining EU action, applying sanctions — and by 2011 the Commission judged Iceland
  **already highly aligned**, so this wasn't seen as a major accession hurdle. (EUR-Lex / COM(2011) 666)
- ⚠️ **Energy: loses the EEA two-pillar buffer** → if an interconnector existed, **ACER (not ESA)** would
  gain direct competence over Icelandic regulators. Inferential (sources describe the present EEA setup),
  but the structural consequence is clear. (Commission; ACER; Univ. of Oslo)
- ✅ **No requirement to create armed forces.** (EPRS 2026)

## GAINS
- ✅ **A second mutual-defence guarantee** (Art. 42(7)) layered on NATO Art. 5 — added redundancy.
- ✅ **A formal seat and vote** in EU foreign/security policy, vs today's "align but don't decide."
- ✅ **Deeper Arctic/North-Atlantic & maritime security cooperation** — though the March 2026 partnership
  already delivers much of this without membership.

## LOSSES / COSTS
- ✅ **Duplication of NATO** — the EU has **no standing collective-defence force** comparable to NATO;
  actual military capability would still depend on the **US/NATO.** (SIPRI; treaty texts)
- ⚠️ **Loss of the energy sovereignty buffer** (ESA → ACER) — but only bites if a subsea interconnector
  is ever built (none planned).
- ✅ **The Atlanticist concern:** historically Iceland feared an EU-led defence policy would **compete
  with and divide NATO**, weakening the Atlantic solidarity its safety depends on. (SIPRI)

## CRISIS RESILIENCE & SUPPLY LINES (follow-up gather, 2026-06-17)

**The striking fact:** Iceland produces ~100% renewable *electricity* but imports **virtually all
liquid fuel** (transport, fishing fleet, aviation; ~15% of primary energy, zero domestic reserves) —
yet, unlike almost every other developed importer, it is bound by **neither the IEA's nor the EU's
90-day oil-reserve rules** and has **no statutory strategic fuel reserve.** This is the clearest
concrete way EU membership would change resilience.

- ✅ **Not an IEA member** — one of ~5 OECD states outside the IEA, so the IEA 90-day oil-stock
  obligation doesn't apply. (IEA membership list) — *corrects the common assumption that it is.*
- ⚠️ **Not bound by the EU oil-stocks Directive 2009/119** either — deemed *not* EEA-relevant, so not
  incorporated into the EEA. *(load-bearing — from EFTA EEA-Lex factsheet snippet; verify live page.)*
- ⚠️ **No statutory strategic oil reserve;** fuel security rests on undisclosed private commercial
  inventories; mandatory stockholding **under government review since 2025.** *(Nordic Energy Research
  snippet; verify.)*
- ✅ **Food is import-dependent** — Iceland doesn't grow enough; imported food/beverages >$967M in 2024
  (~11.5% of merchandise imports); ~57% of all imports come from the EEA (Norway, NL, UK, DK, DE). After
  COVID + Ukraine the govt earmarked **~ISK 2bn over 5 yrs** to raise domestic cereal production. (US
  ITA Trade.gov; OECD 2025; Hagstofa)
- ✅ **Civil protection** via Almannavarnir; the **2023 National Security Policy** (Alþingi 42–0)
  explicitly names food security, energy security and critical-infrastructure resilience. (Govt of Iceland)

**What membership would change here:**
- ✅ **Imposes a binding strategic oil-stock obligation** (Directive 2009/119: 90 days net imports / 61
  days consumption) — closing the current fuel-reserve gap. (EUR-Lex)
- ✅ **Plugs Iceland into EU crisis coordination** — the Internal Market Emergency & Resilience Act
  (IMERA): joint procurement, reserve distribution, priority allocation of scarce crisis goods — buying
  power a 390k-person island can't replicate alone. *Caveat: the crisis phase is triggered by
  qualified-majority vote in Brussels, not Reykjavík.* (Council of the EU)
- ✅ **What stays the same: geography.** No fixed link/pipeline/interconnector is added; the grid stays
  islanded and fuel/goods still arrive by sea/air regardless. (Landsvirkjun)

**Both sides (resilience):** *Pro* — a first-ever binding fuel reserve + EU crisis solidarity/joint
procurement. *Anti* — the EEA already gives single-market supply access and Iceland can build reserves
unilaterally (review underway) without ceding sovereignty; membership duplicates access while adding
CFP exposure over fisheries (its food-export backbone); geography is the real constraint and EU
membership doesn't change it.
